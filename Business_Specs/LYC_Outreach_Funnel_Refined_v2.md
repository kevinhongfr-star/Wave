# LYC Outreach-to-Revenue Funnel: Refined v2

**Original:** 0 → 500 → 50 → 10 → 2 (90 days)
**Refined:** 0 → 500 → 50 → 12 → 3 (90 days, adjusted for China market)

---

## WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

### Conversion math adjusted
- **Original:** 500 → 50 (10%) → 10 (20%) → 2 (20%)
- **Refined:** 500 → 55 (11%) → 12 (22%) → 3 (25%)
- **Why:** 10% outbound→conversation is too optimistic for cold outreach in China (LinkedIn cold response is 2-4%). But Sniper + Trojan Horse buckets pull up the average. Bumping target slightly to create buffer. Closing rate improved to 25% because diagnostic-first model has higher conversion.

### China seasonality accounted for
- **Jul-Aug:** Corporate dead zone. CHROs at retreats, decision-makers on vacation. Use this for relationship-building only, no closing.
- **Sep-Oct:** Golden window. Everyone's back, Q4 budgets being allocated.
- **Nov-Dec:** Budget flush. Companies spending remaining budget. Prime closing window.
- **Refined timeline:** Build pipeline Jul-Aug → Convert Sep-Oct → Close Nov-Dec

### Weiqi bucket restructured
- **Original:** 100 ecosystem players → treated as direct conversion path
- **Refined:** 100 ecosystem players → REFERRAL ENGINE → feeds Sniper bucket
- **Why:** Ecosystem players (PE partners, coaches, deans) don't buy diagnostics. They INTRODUCE you to buyers. Their value is warm intros, not direct pipeline.

## FUNNEL STRUCTURE (REFINED)

```
TIER 1: WARM (150 contacts)
├── Kevin's network (80-100) — Warmth 4-5
├── Podcast guest pipeline (30-50) — Warmth 3-4
└── Referral chain (20-30) — Warmth 5
    │
    ├─→ 60-80 conversations (40-50% response rate)
    │
TIER 2: WARM-COLD (150 contacts)
├── LinkedIn engagement (30-50) — Warmth 3 (they engage with your content)
├── Event attendees (50-80) — Warmth 2-3
└── Sales Navigator ICP match (50-70) — Warmth 2
    │
    ├─→ 20-30 conversations (13-20% response rate)
    │
TIER 3: COLD (200 contacts)
├── Sales Navigator (remaining ICP) — Warmth 1
└── Industry directory scrape — Warmth 1
    │
    ├─→ 10-15 conversations (5-7.5% response rate)
    │
TOTAL: 500 outreach → 90-125 conversations → 55+ real conversations (after qualification)
```

**Key insight:** Don't treat all 500 equally. Warm contacts convert 5-8x better than cold. Front-load warm, use cold to fill pipeline gaps.

## OUTREACH CADENCE (REFINED)

### V1 had 4 touches over 21 days. V2 has 5 touches over 28 days with better spacing.

| Day | Action | Channel | Notes |
|-----|--------|---------|-------|
| Day 0 | First message | LinkedIn / Email / Intro | Personalized per bucket |
| Day 3 | Engage with their content | LinkedIn | Like + thoughtful comment (not generic) |
| Day 7 | Second message (new angle) | LinkedIn / Email | Share relevant insight, not "following up" |
| Day 14 | Third touch — value add | LinkedIn / Email | Send research, article, or podcast episode |
| Day 21 | Final touch — event invite | LinkedIn | Webinar or roundtable invitation |
| Day 28 | STOP outbound → Nurture | — | Auto-add to newsletter, mark as "Nurture" in CRM |

**Why 28 days instead of 21:** China business culture moves slower. 21 days feels pushy. 28 days with genuine value touches feels persistent but respectful.

## OPPORTUNITY SCORING (REFINED)

### V1 was too subjective. V2 uses a weighted scoring matrix.

| Signal | Weight | How to Assess |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Named a specific, painful problem** | 0-3 | 0=generic "we have challenges" / 1=mentions area but vague / 2=names specific issue / 3=describes impact (cost, turnover, timeline) |
| **Budget authority** | 0-3 | 0=no authority / 1=influencer only / 2=can approve <50K / 3=can approve >50K or has direct budget |
| **Timeline urgency** | 0-3 | 0=no urgency / 1="someday" / 2="this quarter" / 3="next 30-60 days" |
| **Product fit** | 0-2 | 0=not a fit / 1=adjacent / 2=direct fit with PRISM/BRIDGE/MOSAIC/SPARK/FORGE |
| **Decision process clarity** | 0-2 | 0=no idea who decides / 1=knows who decides / 2=can get you to the decision-maker |

**Maximum score: 13**

| Score | Classification | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| 10-13 | Hot opportunity | Send proposal within 48 hours. Kevin calls within 24h. |
| 7-9 | Warm opportunity | Nurture 2 weeks, then propose. Invite to webinar first. |
| 4-6 | Early-stage | Full nurture mode. Re-engage in 60 days. |
| 0-3 | Not qualified | Nurture only. Don't waste time. |

## CONTENT FEEDBACK LOOP (REFINED)

### V1 said "every piece of content serves the funnel." V2 maps content to specific buckets.

| Content Type | Primary Bucket | Secondary | CTA |
|--------------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| **LinkedIn thought leadership** | Farmer (attract) | Nurture (retain) | "DM me if this resonates" |
| **Case studies / diagnostic results** | Sniper (credibility) | Farmer (social proof) | "Let's discuss your situation" |
| **Podcast episodes** | Trojan Horse (reciprocity) | Weiqi (guest invites) | "Want to be a guest? DM me" |
| **Research / white papers** | Sniper (authority) | Nurture (expertise signal) | "Let's discuss findings" |
| **Webinar** | Nurture (re-engage) | Farmer (convert) | "Book a 1:1 follow-up" |

| **Newsletter** | Nurture (weekly touch) | Farmer (passive) | "Reply with your challenge" |

**Content production cadence:**
- LinkedIn: 3x/week (Mon/Wed/Fri)
- Newsletter: 1x/week (Thursday)
- Podcast: 1x/week (Tuesday)
- Research: 1x/quarter (big piece)
- Webinar: 1x/month
- Workshop: 1x/quarter

## PIPELINE VELOCITY (NEW — MISSING FROM V1)

### Track average days in each stage. Without this, you can't predict close dates.

| Stage | Target Velocity | Red Flag |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| Outreach → Response | 3-7 days | >14 days (message didn't land or wrong target) |
| Response → Conversation | 3-5 days | >10 days (losing momentum) |
| Conversation → Opportunity | 5-10 days | >21 days (not a real opportunity) |
| Opportunity → Proposal | 3-5 days | >7 days (you're not prioritizing) |
| Proposal → Close | 14-30 days | >45 days (stalled, likely dead) |
| Close → Nurture (if no) | 0 days | — (always moves to nurture, never dead) |

**Weekly pipeline review should flag anything exceeding red flag thresholds.**

## COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT (NEW — MISSING FROM V1)

### What to do when they say "we already work with [competitor]"

| Competitor | Your Angle | Response Template |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Big consulting (McKinsey, BCG) | "We're more specific and faster. They do strategy; we do talent diagnostics + execution." | "Totally understand. Most of our clients worked with [competitor] before. What we do differently is [specific diagnostic] — it gives you actionable data in 2 weeks, not 6 months. Would it be useful to see how it compares?" |
| Local HR consulting | "We bring global frameworks + China execution. They know local; we know local + international." | "Good. We actually complement [local firm] well — they handle [X], we handle [Y]. Happy to explore if there's a gap we can fill." |

| No one (building in-house) | "Building is slow and expensive. We give you the framework + data in weeks, not years." | "Totally respect that. One thing we've seen is that internal teams often lack [benchmarking data / diagnostic rigor / cross-industry insights]. That's where we help. Would a 20-min conversation be useful?" |

- Goal: 3+ paid engagements
