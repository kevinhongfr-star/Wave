**Dimension 2: Intelligence (R × 0.15)**

**Dimension 3: Decision-rights (I × 0.15)**

What it measures: Architecture quality — committees, reporting lines, escalation paths. Whether the governance structure matches the operating reality.

**Dimension 5: Execution (G × 0.15)**
What it measures: Speed-to-decision metrics. Time from decision to action. Whether governance decisions actually get implemented.

## INSTRUCTION 3 OF 4: Advisory Discovery Workshop Deck — Governance Architecture (Force 1)

**To Notion AI:** Create a 20-25 slide workshop deck for the opening discovery session in a Force 1 advisory engagement. This is presented at the first working session after the engagement is signed.

**Format:** Slide-by-slide content with facilitator notes and timing.

**Reference:** Page 06 (BRIDGE): https://www.notion.so/398fafae51be8100bcf7d4512196f414

**SLIDE STRUCTURE:**

**Slides 1-3: FRAMING**
- Slide 1: Title — "Governance Architecture Discovery Workshop" | Client name, date, "Confidential"
- Slide 2: Engagement objectives — what we're here to accomplish in this session
- Slide 3: Agenda + ground rules — "This is a working session, not a presentation. We need your candor."

**Slides 4-6: CONTEXT SETTING**
- Slide 4: The China Realignment thesis — "Your governance architecture was designed for a world that no longer exists." Data on cross-border governance failure rates (70%+ of cross-border initiatives underperform due to governance gaps).
- Slide 5: The Three Forces framework — brief overview. "Today we focus on Force 1: governance architecture."
- Slide 6: BRIDGE diagnostic overview — "We've already collected data on your governance health. Today's workshop puts context around that data."

**Slides 7-12: WORKSHOP EXERCISES**
- Slide 7: Exercise 1 — Governance Structure Audit. "Map your current governance architecture: board composition, committee structure, decision-rights matrix, reporting lines. Where does the structure match reality? Where does it diverge?"
- Slide 8: Facilitator guide for Exercise 1 — 20 min, small groups, flipchart/template. Key questions to probe.
- Slide 9: Exercise 2 — Decision Speed Test. "For the last 5 major decisions: who initiated, who approved, who was consulted, how long did it take, what was the outcome? Map the actual vs. intended decision flow."
- Slide 10: Facilitator guide for Exercise 2 — 25 min, individual reflection then group discussion. Decision types: investment, hiring, market entry, org change, crisis response.
- Slide 11: Exercise 3 — Information Asymmetry Check. "Rate (1-5) how well-informed each layer of your organization is about: strategy, market conditions, competitor moves, board priorities. Where are the information gaps?"
- Slide 12: Facilitator guide for Exercise 3 — 15 min, anonymous voting then reveal.

**Slides 13-16: DIAGNOSTIC INTEGRATION**
- Slide 13: "Now let's connect this to your BRIDGE data." Reveal governance health scores (if pre-assessment completed) or preview what the diagnostic will measure.
- Slide 14: BRIDGE dimensions explained — Board Readiness (×0.20), Intelligence (×0.15), Decision-rights (×0.15), Governance (×0.20), Execution (×0.15). What each means in practice.
- Slide 15: Benchmark data — "Organizations at your stage typically score [X] on governance health. Here's where you're likely to land based on today's exercise."
- Slide 16: The governance maturity model — 5 levels (Ad Hoc → Reactive → Structured → Optimized → Self-Correcting). Where does your organization sit today? Where does it need to be in 2 years?

**Slides 17-19: SYNTHESIS & NEXT STEPS**
- Slide 17: Key themes from today's exercises — facilitator summarizes in real-time. "We heard [X themes]. Here's how they map to the BRIDGE dimensions."
- Slide 18: Proposed engagement milestones — "Based on today, here's what the next 90 days look like." BRIDGE full administration, debrief, advisory engagement design, first quarterly review.
- Slide 19: Action items — specific next steps with owners and dates.

**Slide 20: CLOSE**
- "Thank you for the candor. The data from today + your BRIDGE assessment gives us the foundation for everything that follows."
- Contact info, next session date.
