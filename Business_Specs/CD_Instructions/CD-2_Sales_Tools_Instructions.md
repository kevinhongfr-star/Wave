## INSTRUCTION 1 OF 6: Objection Handling Playbook

**To Notion AI:** Create the full Objection Handling Playbook — a structured guide for 15 common objections. Each objection has a 5-part response framework.

**Format:** Notion document with a table of contents and 15 sections.

**Response Framework (5 parts per objection):**
1. Objection verbatim (quoted exactly as a prospect would say it)
2. Why They Say It (1 sentence — the real concern behind the words)
3. Reframe (2-3 sentences, McKinsey tone, ALWAYS ends with a question)
4. Evidence Point (specific diagnostic data, product fact, or ROI number)
5. Next Question (moves the conversation forward — never defensive)

**Tone rules:** Never defensive. Always curious. Data over opinions. Reframes max 2-3 sentences. Every reframe ends with a question.

**15 OBJECTIONS — Full content:**

### PRICING (5 objections)

**O1: "That's too expensive for an assessment."**
- Why: They're comparing to cheap online quizzes, not the business impact of the data.
- Reframe: "The assessment itself isn't the investment — the decision it informs is. A single promotion decision in your organization likely costs $500K+ in total compensation over 3 years. The question isn't whether the assessment is expensive, but whether the decision it supports is worth getting right. What's the cost of the last leadership decision you wish you had better data for?"
- Evidence: SPARK at $500-$800 informs a $375K leadership development decision. BRIDGE at $1,200-$2,000 informs cross-border mandates worth millions.
- Next question: "What would a 20% improvement in your cross-border decision speed be worth to your board?"

**O2: "We already have an assessment tool."**
- Why: They don't see the differentiation — they think all assessments measure the same thing.
- Reframe: "Most leadership assessments measure traits. Ours measures operational readiness for the three forces reshaping leadership right now — governance complexity, cross-border partnership dynamics, and AI-driven transformation. They answer different questions. The question is whether your current tool measures what your board actually needs answered. What does your current assessment tell you about your leaders' readiness for AI transformation?"
- Evidence: SPARK measures 5 AI-specific signals (Literacy, Governance Judgment, Strategic Capability, Behavioral Adaptability, Team & Culture). BRIDGE measures 6 cross-border governance dimensions. FORGE measures revenue architecture. These don't exist in generic 360 tools.
- Next question: "If your current tool doesn't measure AI readiness or governance architecture, what are you missing?"

**O3: "A $5K program seems high."**
- Why: They're comparing to a conference ticket, not to the cost of the problem the program solves.
- Reframe: "A $5K investment for a senior leader represents less than 2% of their annual compensation. If the program prevents one failed leadership transition — which costs $500K+ — the ROI is 100x. The real question is what it costs your organization if this leader's gap goes unaddressed. What's the downside of inaction?"
- Evidence: SHIFT-QUEST at $5,000/person over 6 months (12 sessions). Average leadership failure cost: $500K+ in disruption. ROI: 100x if it prevents one failed transition.
- Next question: "What would you invest to ensure your next leadership transition succeeds?"

**O4: "We have no budget for advisory retainers right now."**
- Why: They may need advisory most when they feel they can't afford it — the problems compound.
- Reframe: "Budget conversations usually mean timing conversations. The governance and partnership challenges you're navigating today don't pause because the budget cycle hasn't caught up. If we can structure a starting point — say, Tier 1 at $3,000/month for one quarter — would that make the conversation possible now? What happens if these challenges go another 6 months without strategic support?"
- Evidence: Tier 1 starts at $3,000/month. Includes 2 sessions/month + monthly brief + email access. 90-day commitment, no lock-in beyond the quarter.
- Next question: "Would a one-quarter engagement to address your most urgent priority make sense as a starting point?"

**O5: "Your competitor charges less."**
- Why: They're comparing price without comparing scope, methodology, or outcomes.
- Reframe: "Price differences in advisory usually reflect differences in methodology depth and diagnostic integration. Our model is built on proprietary diagnostics that measure specific operational forces — governance architecture, partnership dynamics, AI readiness — not general leadership traits. Before comparing price, it's worth asking what each approach actually measures and what outcomes it delivers. Would it be helpful to compare what you'd get from each approach?"
- Evidence: LYC includes diagnostic-first methodology, proprietary assessments (SPARK, BRIDGE, MOSAIC, FORGE), McKinsey-grade reporting, Invitation Council access.
- Next question: "What matters more to your board — the cheapest advisory engagement, or the one that actually moves your governance metrics?"

### RELEVANCE (4 objections)

**O6: "We don't do business in China — BRIDGE isn't relevant."**
- Why: They're interpreting BRIDGE as China-specific rather than cross-border governance.
- Reframe: "BRIDGE isn't about China — it's about any cross-border governance architecture. If your leadership team spans more than one country, or your board makes decisions affecting operations in a different market than your HQ, the governance dimensions BRIDGE measures are already at play. How many decision-makers in your org are in a different location from the stakeholders they serve?"
- Evidence: BRIDGE measures 6 governance dimensions: Board Readiness (×0.20), Intelligence (×0.15), Decision-rights (×0.15), Governance (×0.20), Execution (×0.15). Applicable to any multi-market governance structure.
- Next question: "Even within your current markets, are your decision-rights clear enough that cross-border decisions don't stall?"

**O7: "Our team is all in one country — MOSAIC doesn't apply."**
- Why: They think MOSAIC is only for culturally diverse teams.
- Reframe: "MOSAIC measures operating-norms alignment and style calibration — which matter even in homogenous teams when functions disagree. Sales and Operations in the same city can have more 'cultural' friction than a team spanning two countries. How aligned are your leadership team's operating norms today?"
- Evidence: MOSAIC measures: Management (×0.25), Operating-norms (×0.20), Style-calibration (×0.20), Alignment (×0.20), Intercultural-cohesion (×0.15). The intercultural dimension is one of five — the others apply to any team.
- Next question: "What does misalignment cost your team in meeting time and decision delays each quarter?"

**O8: "We're not a sales organization — FORGE isn't for us."**
- Why: They think FORGE is only for VP Sales roles.
- Reframe: "FORGE measures revenue architecture — which applies to any organization where revenue depends on predictable forecasting, operational execution, and growth enablement. If your board asks 'why did we miss forecast?' you already need FORGE. How confident are you in your revenue organization's ability to hit next quarter's number?"
- Evidence: FORGE measures: Forecasting (×0.20), Operations (×0.20), Revenue-architecture (×0.25), Growth-execution (×0.12), Growth-enablement (×0.08), Enablement (×0.15).
- Next question: "When was the last time your revenue forecast was accurate within 5%?"

**O9: "AI isn't a priority for us yet."**
- Why: They think AI readiness is a future problem, not a current one.
- Reframe: "AI readiness isn't about whether you've adopted AI — it's about whether your leadership team has the literacy, judgment, and behavioral adaptability to lead through AI adoption when the time comes. The organizations that struggle with AI aren't the ones that adopted late — they're the ones whose leaders weren't ready when they did. What's your leadership team's current AI literacy level?"
- Evidence: SPARK measures 5 AI readiness signals: AI Literacy, Governance Judgment, Strategic Capability, Behavioral Adaptability, Team & Culture.
- Next question: "If your board asked tomorrow 'is our leadership team ready for AI transformation,' could you answer with data?"

### TIMING (3 objections)

**O10: "We're not ready — maybe next quarter."**
- Why: They're overwhelmed or waiting for a trigger event.
- Reframe: "Timing pressure often signals urgency — if it wasn't important, you wouldn't be having this conversation. The governance and leadership challenges you're navigating today don't pause for quarter boundaries. Would it help to start with a focused diagnostic to quantify exactly where things stand, so you can enter next quarter with data instead of assumptions?"
- Evidence: SPARK takes 15-25 minutes. A focused diagnostic debrief takes 45-60 minutes. The insight gap closes faster with data.
- Next question: "What would change if you had diagnostic data on your leadership gaps before next quarter's planning cycle?"

**O11: "Let me think about it."**
- Why: They need to process, or they need something they haven't asked for.
- Reframe: "Absolutely — these are significant decisions. What would be most helpful while you're thinking: a one-page summary of your specific diagnostic results and recommendations, or a conversation with a peer who's already gone through this process?"
- Evidence: LYC provides personalized debrief reports after every diagnostic. Peer reference available through Invitation Council.
- Next question: "What's the one thing that, if we could clarify it right now, would make this an easy decision?"

**O12: "I need to check with my board/CEO."**
- Why: They may not have decision authority, or they need ammunition to advocate internally.
- Reframe: "That's the right process. To make that conversation easier for you, would it help to have a one-page brief you can forward — summarizing your diagnostic findings, the cost of inaction, and the proposed investment with expected ROI? We can prepare that for you today."
- Evidence: LYC can prepare a Decision Brief document for internal stakeholders. ROI data from calculator supports the business case.
- Next question: "What does your CEO/board care most about right now — governance risk, talent pipeline, or revenue predictability?"

### AUTHORITY/SCOPE (3 objections)

**O13: "I'm not the decision-maker."**
- Why: They may be the champion but not the signer.
- Reframe: "Thank you for being transparent. Often the person having the initial conversation is the one who understands the problem best. Would it be helpful if we prepared a briefing document you could share with the decision-maker, framing the issue and the proposed solution in terms they'd respond to?"
- Evidence: LYC Decision Briefs are designed for exactly this purpose — translating diagnostic insight into executive language.
- Next question: "Who in your organization would need to see this data to move forward?"

**O14: "We need something for the whole organization, not just one team."**
- Why: They see the value but think the scope is too narrow.
- Reframe: "That's exactly the right instinct — and it's why we built our diagnostics to scale from individual to team to organizational level. We can start with a focused diagnostic on your most critical team, then expand the approach org-wide based on what we learn. Most enterprise partnerships start this way. What's the most urgent leadership challenge your organization faces right now?"
- Evidence: Bundle #7 (Enterprise Partnership): FORGE + 12-month Advisory + Council access at $110,000. Includes organizational-level diagnostics and custom intelligence reports.
- Next question: "If we started with your most critical team and built a case for broader deployment, would that be a path worth exploring?"

**O15: "How is this different from what our L&D team already does?"**
- Why: They see overlap with internal capabilities.
- Reframe: "L&D teams build capability — we diagnose where capability gaps exist and measure whether interventions are working. Most L&D teams tell us our diagnostic data is exactly what they've been missing: evidence-based baselines that make their programs more targeted and measurable. Think of it as giving your L&D team the MRI before they prescribe the treatment. What data does your L&D team currently use to diagnose leadership gaps?"
- Evidence: LYC diagnostics are diagnostic-first (not training-first). They integrate with existing L&D programs rather than replacing them. SHIFT programs complement internal L&D.
- Next question: "If your L&D team had diagnostic data on every senior leader's readiness for the Three Forces, how would that change your development priorities?"

## INSTRUCTION 4 OF 6: Diagnostic Debrief Call Guide

**To Notion AI:** Create the full Diagnostic Debrief Call Guide — a structured facilitation guide for post-diagnostic debrief calls (45-60 minutes). This is the most critical sales conversation: where diagnostic results translate into engagement decisions.

**Format:** Notion document with timing, scripts, and decision trees.

**CALL STRUCTURE (4 phases):**

**PHASE 1: CONTEXT SETTING (5 min)**
- Script: "Thank you for completing the [diagnostic name]. Before we walk through the results, I want to set expectations. You'll see a score profile that shows your [readiness/cohesion/health] across [X dimensions]. Some areas will be strong — that's your foundation. Others will show gaps — that's where the opportunity is. We'll close with specific recommendations and what to do next. Any questions before we begin?"
- Check: Confirm they understand this is a conversation, not a presentation. They can interrupt, ask questions, challenge the data.
- Logistics: Screen share ready, report open, ROI calculator ready (if pricing comes up).

**PHASE 2: RESULTS WALKTHROUGH (20-25 min)**
- Start with the COMPOSITE SCORE — "Your overall [readiness/cohesion/health] score is [X/100]. Here's what that means in practical terms."
- Then walk through EACH DIMENSION (1-2 min per dimension):
  - Score visualization (radar chart reference)
  - Green (≥70) / Amber (40-69) / Red (<40) indicator
  - 2-3 specific observations per dimension
  - Benchmark comparison: "You're [above/below/at] the benchmark for [peer group]"
  - Ask after each: "Does this match what you're seeing on the ground?"
- For RED dimensions: probe deeper. "This is the area that stands out most. Help me understand what's happening here."
- For GREEN dimensions: validate. "This is clearly a strength. What's working that you'd want to protect?"
- Cross-force connections (if applicable): "Your BRIDGE score shows [X]. This connects directly to your MOSAIC results showing [Y]. The gap between leader readiness and team cohesion is where partnership friction compounds."

**PHASE 3: IMPLICATIONS & COST OF INACTION (10 min)**
- Script: "Let me put this in business terms. Based on what the data shows..."
- For each red/amber dimension: quantify the cost. Use ROI calculator data.
- "If nothing changes, here's what the trajectory looks like over the next 12 months."
- Cross-sell trigger identification:
  - If SPARK → 2+ signal gaps → recommend coaching
  - If BRIDGE → governance gaps → recommend advisory Tier 1
  - If MOSAIC → team friction → recommend team workshop
  - If FORGE → revenue architecture weakness → recommend advisory + workshop
- Script transition: "The question is: what do you want to do with this information?"

**PHASE 4: NEXT STEPS & CLOSE (10 min)**
- Present 2-3 options (not 1):
  - Option A (Diagnostic only): "You have the data. You can work with your internal team to address the gaps."
  - Option B (Diagnostic + Advisory): "We embed with you monthly to act on this data. Starting at $3,000/month."
  - Option C (Diagnostic + Program): "You join a cohort of leaders working on the same challenges. $5,000 for SHIFT-QUEST."
- "Which of these feels most aligned with where you are right now?"
- If hesitation: offer a "thinking session" — "Would it help to have a 30-minute follow-up next week to think through the options? No commitment."
- Document the agreed next step in real-time. Send follow-up within 24 hours.

**CROSS-SELL TRIGGERS (quick reference):**
| Diagnostic Result | Trigger | Cross-sell | Conversation Starter |
|---|---|---|---|
| SPARK: 2+ signal gaps | At debrief | Coaching (SHIFT-LEAP) | "These gaps are exactly what our coaching program addresses. Would you like to see what a 12-session arc looks like for someone with your profile?" |
| BRIDGE: governance <40 | At debrief | Advisory Tier 1 | "This governance gap needs ongoing advisory support, not a one-time fix. Let me show you what a quarterly engagement looks like." |
| MOSAIC: team score <50 | At debrief | Team Workshop | "Your team's cohesion score suggests a facilitated workshop would accelerate alignment. We run these quarterly." |
| FORGE: revenue arch <40 | At debrief | Advisory + Workshop | "Your revenue architecture needs both strategic advisory and a structured diagnostic workshop. Let me show you how Bundle #4 works." |
