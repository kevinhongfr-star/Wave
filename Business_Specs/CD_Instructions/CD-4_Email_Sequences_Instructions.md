## INSTRUCTION 1 OF 5: Cold Outreach Sequence — Forum Prospect (3 Emails)

**To Notion AI:** Create a 3-email cold outreach sequence for prospects who are candidates for the Executive Forum. These are senior leaders who haven't engaged with LYC before.

**Target persona:** CHRO, CEO, Board Director, Senior VP at companies with cross-border operations ($500M+ revenue) or undergoing AI transformation.

**SEQUENCE:**

**Email 1 — Day 0: "The Three Forces Reshaping Leadership"**
Subject: The three forces reshaping leadership in [their industry/region]
Body: Open with a specific, data-driven observation about their market. Not "I noticed your company..." Instead: "Leadership teams in [industry] are facing a convergence: governance complexity is increasing, AI is dissolving traditional operating models, and the speed gap between board decisions and execution is widening. We've been tracking how [specific trend] affects organizations at your stage. [One sentence on what we've found.] I'm putting together a brief on [specific topic relevant to their role]. Would it be useful?"
CTA: "Reply 'yes' and I'll send it over. No commitment."
Word count: 80-100 words.

**Email 2 — Day 4: "A specific insight"**
Subject: [Specific data point] — what it means for [their function]
Body: Share one genuine insight from the 500 pain points database or diagnostic data. Not a pitch. "We recently completed [X] assessments across [Y] organizations. One finding stands out: [specific data point, e.g., '72% of cross-border leadership teams have decision-rights clarity below 40/100 — yet 89% of boards believe their governance structure is adequate.']. The gap between perception and data is where the risk lives. This connects directly to what we're seeing in [their market]. I'm hosting a 30-minute webinar on [topic] on [date]. Would [specific person] be interested?"
CTA: "Here's the link: [URL]. Takes 30 minutes. No pitch at the end — just data and discussion."
Word count: 100-120 words.

**Email 3 — Day 8: "A peer introduction"**
Subject: [Mutual connection or peer example]
Body: "A [CEO/CHRO] at a [similar company profile] recently told us: '[specific quote or paraphrase about their challenge].' After running our [diagnostic name], they found [specific finding]. They're now working with us on [specific intervention]. I'm not suggesting your situation is the same — but if [specific challenge] resonates, I'd welcome a 20-minute conversation. No diagnostic required. Just a conversation."
CTA: "Worth 20 minutes? Here's my calendar: [link]."
Word count: 80-100 words.

## INSTRUCTION 2 OF 5: Webinar Nurture Sequence — Post-Registration (4 Emails)

**To Notion AI:** Create a 4-email sequence for people who just registered for an LYC webinar. Goal: maximize attendance and prepare them for the post-webinar conversion.

**SEQUENCE:**

**Email 1 — Immediately: Confirmation**
Subject: You're registered: [Webinar Title] — [Date]
Body: Confirm registration. "Here's what you'll walk away with: [3 specific takeaways]. To prepare: [one optional pre-read or reflection question]. We'll send a reminder 24 hours before."
CTA: "Add to calendar: [link]."
Word count: 60-80 words.

**Email 2 — Day before: Context**
Subject: Tomorrow: [specific question the webinar answers]
Body: Share one insight or data point related to the webinar topic. "Ahead of tomorrow's session, one data point: [specific finding]. This is what we'll be digging into — why [X] happens and what the data shows about organizations that solve it."
CTA: "If you have a specific question you want addressed, reply to this email. We'll try to cover it."
Word count: 80-100 words.

**Email 3 — 2 hours before: Reminder + Urgency**
Subject: Starting in 2 hours: [Webinar Title]
Body: "We're going live at [time]. Here's the link: [URL]. [One sentence on the most provocative thing they'll hear.]"
CTA: "Join here: [link]. See you in 2 hours."
Word count: 40-50 words.

**Email 4 — Day after: Replay + Next Step**
Subject: Replay: [Webinar Title] + the data behind [key insight]
Body: "Thank you for joining [yesterday/earlier]. For those who couldn't make it: replay link is here [URL]. For those who attended: the diagnostic we mentioned — [diagnostic name] — is available for [pricing]. It takes [X minutes] and you'll get [specific output]. If you attended and want to discuss your results, book a 20-minute debrief here: [link]."
CTA: Two options: "Watch the replay: [link]" OR "Book a debrief: [link]"
Word count: 100-120 words.

## INSTRUCTION 3 OF 5: Diagnostic Prospect Sequence (4 Emails)

**To Notion AI:** Create a 4-email sequence for prospects who've shown interest in diagnostics (e.g., after webinar, after discovery call, after content download).

**Target:** Leaders who understand they have a problem but haven't committed to assessment.

**SEQUENCE:**

**Email 1 — Day 0: "The data gap"**
Subject: What your board doesn't know about [specific governance/talent/AI gap]
Body: Reference the conversation or content they engaged with. "Following our conversation about [topic], one thing I wanted to share: [specific data point from diagnostic research]. Most organizations we work with discover that their perception of [governance health/team cohesion/AI readiness] diverges significantly from the data. The gap is where the risk lives."
CTA: "The [diagnostic name] takes [X minutes] and gives you a score across [Y dimensions]. Here's what it measures: [link to diagnostics page]. Worth exploring?"
Word count: 80-100.

**Email 2 — Day 3: "What the data reveals"**
Subject: [Specific dimension] — where most leaders underestimate the gap
Body: Share a specific finding pattern. "In our last [X] assessments, the most common finding was: [specific insight]. Leaders consistently rate their [dimension] at [X], while the data shows [Y]. The pattern is consistent across [industries/regions]. What's interesting is that the gap isn't in [unexpected dimension] — it's in [expected dimension] that everyone assumes is fine."
CTA: "Curious where your organization lands? The [diagnostic] takes [X minutes]. [Link]"
Word count: 100-120.

**Email 3 — Day 7: "A client example"**
Subject: How [anonymized client] used diagnostic data to [specific outcome]
Body: Brief anonymized case study. "A [role] at a [company type] came to us saying [problem]. After completing the [diagnostic], the data showed [finding]. The [dimension] score was [X] — below where they thought, above where they feared on [other dimension]. The insight that changed everything: [specific realization]. Within [timeframe], they [specific action and outcome]."
CTA: "Would a similar diagnostic conversation be useful? Here's my calendar: [link]. 20 minutes, no commitment."
Word count: 100-120.

**Email 4 — Day 12: "Final thought"**
Subject: One question before we close the loop
Body: "I don't want to over-communicate, so this will be my last note on this. One question: if you could have perfect data on one aspect of your leadership organization — [governance readiness / team cohesion / AI preparedness / revenue architecture] — which would it be? If you reply with which one, I'll send you the relevant diagnostic overview and a specific example of how it's been used in your industry."
CTA: "Just reply with the one area. I'll take it from there."
Word count: 80-100.

## INSTRUCTION 4 OF 5: B2B Nurture Email Sequence (5 Emails)

**To Notion AI:** Create a 5-email nurture sequence for B2B prospects who've downloaded content, attended a webinar, or had an initial conversation but haven't progressed to a diagnostic or advisory engagement.

**Cadence:** Weekly (every 7 days)

**Email 1: "The problem you're probably not measuring"**
- Focus: Most leadership challenges go undiagnosed because organizations don't have the measurement tools.
- Content: Share a framework (Three Forces) and ask them to self-identify which force is most pressing.
- CTA: "Which force resonates most? Reply and I'll send relevant data."

**Email 2: "What [X] assessments told us about [their challenge]"**
- Focus: Data-driven insight relevant to their likely pain point.
- Content: One specific finding from diagnostic data with practical implications.
- CTA: "Want to see where your organization lands? Here's the diagnostic overview: [link]."

**Email 3: "The cost of waiting"**
- Focus: Quantified cost of inaction.
- Content: ROI calculator data. "If [problem] goes unaddressed for 12 months, the projected cost is [X]. A targeted diagnostic costs [Y]. The math is straightforward."
- CTA: "Book a 20-minute diagnostic discovery call: [link]."

**Email 4: "What a peer did"**
- Focus: Anonymized case study with specific outcomes.
- Content: Challenge → Diagnostic → Intervention → Result.
- CTA: "Would a similar approach work for your situation? Let's talk: [link]."

**Email 5: "Last note — a resource"**
- Focus: Give value, then step back.
- Content: Share a useful resource (one-pager, brief, framework) with no ask.
- CTA: "Here's a resource we put together: [link]. No follow-up from me — but if you ever want to pick up the conversation, my door's open."
