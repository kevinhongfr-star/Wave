## INSTRUCTION 1 OF 5: Invitation Council — Membership Charter

**To Notion AI:** Create the Invitation Council Membership Charter — the foundational document defining what the Council is, who it's for, how it operates, and what members receive.

**Reference Page 11 (Council Intelligence): https://www.notion.so/398fafae51be81dd8c77f43d2fcb448d**

**Format:** 5-8 page Notion document.

**DOCUMENT STRUCTURE:**

**1. What Is the Invitation Council? (1p)**
- A curated, invitation-only community of senior leaders navigating the Three Forces: governance complexity (Force 1), cross-border partnership dynamics (Force 2), and AI-driven organizational transformation (Force 3).
- NOT a networking group. NOT a mastermind. NOT a paid membership.
- It's a strategic intelligence community — members contribute real-world leadership challenges and receive diagnostic-grade insight from peers and LYC advisors.
- Tagline: "Lead With the Best Minds in the Room"

**2. Why It Exists (1p)**
- The three forces reshaping leadership don't respect industry boundaries. A CHRO in manufacturing faces the same governance architecture challenges as a CHRO in financial services.
- Traditional peer networks are too broad (general business networking) or too narrow (industry-specific conferences).
- The Council fills the gap: cross-industry, evidence-based, diagnostic-informed strategic intelligence.
- "The quality of your leadership decisions is constrained by the quality of the minds you have access to."

**3. Who Gets Invited (1p)**
- Seniority: C-suite, Board Directors, Senior VPs with P&L responsibility
- Challenge: Actively navigating one or more of the Three Forces
- Mindset: Evidence-based, willing to share real challenges (not just success stories), committed to diagnostic thinking
- Geography: Currently APAC-focused, expanding to global
- Size: Initial cohort limited to 25-30 members to maintain quality
- Selection criteria: not based on company size or revenue alone — based on the quality of the leadership challenge and the willingness to engage transparently

**4. What Members Receive (1-2p)**
- Quarterly Council Meetings: Half-day, curated peer discussion on specific Three Forces topics. Chatham House Rules. Facilitated by LYC senior advisors.
- Diagnostic Access: Complimentary or discounted access to LYC diagnostics (SPARK, BRIDGE, MOSAIC, FORGE) for personal and team use.
- Advisory Priority: Council members get priority scheduling for advisory engagements. Tier 2+ advisory clients automatically qualify for Council membership.
- Intelligence Briefs: Monthly data-driven briefs on governance, cross-border, and AI leadership trends — not thought leadership, but diagnostic data from actual assessments.
- Peer Advisory: Direct access to other Council members for informal strategic advice.
- Annual Half-Day: Annual in-person gathering (more strategic, less social) with guest speakers and diagnostic data releases.

**5. How It Works (1p)**
- Invitation only: Members are identified through diagnostic engagements, advisory relationships, webinar participation, and peer referral.
- No application process — if you're invited, you're invited.
- Annual review: Membership is reviewed annually. Active participation expected.
- Confidentiality: All Council discussions are under Chatham House Rules.

**6. The Inaugural Cohort (1/2p)**
- Target launch: [Q3 2026]
- Target size: 25-30 founding members
- First meeting: [Date/Location TBD]
- Founding member commitment: Attend first meeting, participate in one diagnostic, contribute one case study or challenge for peer discussion.

## INSTRUCTION 4 OF 5: Discovery Call Script — B2B (CHRO & L&D Sponsor)

**To Notion AI:** Create a complete discovery call script for B2B prospects (CHRO, Head of L&D, HR Business Partners). This is the initial 30-minute call to qualify and diagnose fit.

**Format:** Notion document with scripted sections and branching logic.

**CALL STRUCTURE (4 segments, 30 minutes):**

**Segment 1: RAPPORT + CONTEXT (5 min)**
- Script: "Thanks for taking the time. Before we dive in, I want to make sure this is useful for you. I'd like to understand [their challenge], share what we're seeing in the data, and if there's a fit, explore how we might help. If there's no fit, I'll tell you upfront. Sound fair?"
- Transition: "Tell me about what's driving this conversation. What changed recently that made you reach out?"

**Segment 2: DISCOVERY (15 min)**
- Core questions (ask in order, probe on answers):
  1. "What's the most expensive leadership decision your organization is facing right now?"
  2. "How are you currently making that decision? What data do you have?"
  3. "If you could measure one thing about your leadership organization with perfect accuracy, what would it be?"
  4. "Have you used leadership diagnostics before? What was the experience?"
  5. "What would a successful outcome from this conversation look like for you?"
  6. "Who else needs to be involved in any decision about moving forward?"
  7. "What's the timeline pressure here?"
- For each answer: acknowledge, probe deeper, connect to Three Forces where relevant.
- Diagnostic trigger identification:
  - If they mention governance/board/decision-making → BRIDGE
  - If they mention cross-cultural/cross-border/partnership → MOSAIC
  - If they mention AI/transformation/future skills → SPARK
  - If they mention revenue/sales/forecasting → FORGE
  - If they mention team dynamics/alignment → MOSAIC team assessment
  - If they mention succession/talent pipeline → SHIFT Composite

**Segment 3: FRAME (5 min)**
- Script: "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: [restatement]. The Three Forces lens suggests [which force(s) are most relevant]. The data we typically see in similar organizations is [specific data point]. Does that resonate?"
- If fit: "We have a diagnostic that measures exactly this. It takes [X minutes] and gives you [specific output]. Would it be useful to see your data?"
- If no fit: "I appreciate you sharing. Based on what you've described, I think [alternative recommendation]. Would it be helpful if I introduced you to [person/resource]?"

**Segment 4: NEXT STEPS (5 min)**
- If diagnostic fit: "Here's what I'd suggest: complete the [diagnostic name] this week — it takes [X minutes]. I'll send you the link. Once you have results, we schedule a 45-minute debrief where I walk you through the findings. If there's a clear path forward, we discuss engagement options. If not, you keep the data. Either way, you walk away with insight. Does that work?"
- If advisory fit: "I'd like to prepare a brief proposal based on today's conversation. I'll have it to you by [date]. In the meantime, is there any data or context I should factor in?"
- Close: "Thank you for the conversation. I'll send [specific follow-up] within [timeframe]. Best way to reach you: [confirm]."
