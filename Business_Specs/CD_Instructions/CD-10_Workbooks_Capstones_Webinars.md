## CHAT 1 OF 8: Participant Workbook — Governance Track

**To Notion AI:** Build the complete 30-40 page Participant Workbook for the Governance Track. This is a FILL-IN document — not a reading document. Landscape orientation with tables, rating scales, and diagram templates.

**Structure:**

**Pages 1-2: Program Overview**
- Governance Track objectives, 4-module overview, expectations
- Certification criteria: attend all 4 modules + complete workbook + submit capstone + achieve minimum B
- Participant agreement: confidentiality, peer group norms, attendance commitment

**Pages 3-10: Module 1 Workbook (Governance Foundations)**
- Pre-module reflection prompt: "What does governance mean in your organization? Write 3 sentences."
- Exercise 1: Governance Structure Map — fill-in org chart template with boxes for: Board, Executive Committee, Regional Councils, Functional Heads. Lines for reporting relationships. Annotations for decision forums.
- Exercise 2: Decision Speed Test — table with columns: Decision | Who Decided | How Long It Took | Bottleneck Type (information/authority/alignment) | Impact of Delay. Pre-filled row as example.
- Exercise 3: Governance Maturity Self-Assessment — rating table: 5 dimensions × 5 maturity levels. Participant rates their org on each.
- Reflection: "What surprised me?" "What questions do I have for Module 2?"

**Pages 11-18: Module 2 Workbook (Architecture Design)**
- BRIDGE Results Reflection: "My scores: B=[ ] R=[ ] I=[ ] D=[ ] G=[ ] E=[ ]. What surprises me: [ ]. What confirms my intuition: [ ]."
- Exercise 4: Governance Canvas — one-page visual template. Boxes for: Committees, Reporting Lines, Escalation Paths, Information Flows. Participant fills in their current architecture.
- Exercise 5: Decision-Rights Matrix — RACI table for top 10 decisions. Columns: Decision | Recommends | Agrees | Commits | Informed | Notes. Separate columns for HQ vs Local.
- Exercise 6: Localization Spectrum — table: Decision Domain | Current Authority | Recommended Authority | Gap | Action. Domains: Strategy, Operations, Talent, Finance, Compliance.
- Draft: Governance Architecture Redesign Proposal (1-page template with sections: Current State, Target State, Key Changes, Implementation Approach).

**Pages 19-26: Module 3 Workbook (Decision Speed & Localization)**
- Exercise 7: Decision Speed Audit — table: Top 5 Slowest Decisions | Current Speed | Target Speed | Bottleneck Root Cause | Intervention Needed | Status.
- Exercise 8: Localization Framework Design — for 3 decision domains: design the localization approach (centralize/decentralize/co-decide), rationale, stakeholders, risks.
- Reflection: "Where am I the bottleneck?" — personal leadership governance check. 5 open-ended prompts.
- Implementation Log: "One decision acceleration intervention I tried: [ ]. Result: [ ]. What I learned: [ ]."

**Pages 27-34: Module 4 Workbook (Board Alignment & Measurement)**
- Exercise 9: Board Pack Redesign — template for redesigning board reporting using the framework. Sections: Current board pack contents, proposed changes, rationale.
- Exercise 10: Governance KPI Dashboard — table: 10 KPIs organized by BRIDGE dimensions | Current State (score/data) | Target State | Owner | Review Cadence.
- Draft: 90-Day Governance Improvement Plan — template: 3 improvements × actions × owners × milestones × success metrics × review dates.
- Capstone Project Progress Tracker: Milestone 1 (Week 1-2) | Milestone 2 (Week 3) | Final (Week 4). Columns: What I did | What I learned | What's next | Blockers.

**Pages 35-38: Capstone Project Section**
- Project brief (reference CD-10 Chat 4 for full brief)
- Progress log (weekly check-in template)
- Peer feedback forms (2-3 peers, structured: strength, gap, recommendation)
- Final submission checklist

**Pages 39-40: Program Reflection**
- Pre/post BRIDGE comparison (if re-assessed): dimension-by-dimension delta
- "What I learned about governance in my organization" (open-ended)
- "What I'll do differently starting Monday" (open-ended)
- Commitments for next 90 days (3 specific commitments with accountability partner)
