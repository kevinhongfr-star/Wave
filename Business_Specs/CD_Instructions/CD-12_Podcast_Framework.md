### TRACK A: "The Governance Shift" (12 episodes)

**A-01: "The Governance Illusion"**
- Key Question: Why do 70% of organizations think their governance works when it doesn't?
- Core Insight: The self-assessment gap. Leaders overestimate governance maturity by 1.3 points on a 5-point scale. The gap is widest in Decision-rights and Intelligence.
- Data: Self-assessed 3.4/5.0 vs actual 2.1/5.0.
- One Thing: Ask your board: "When was the last time we measured governance health — not financial performance?"

**A-02: "Decision Speed Is Your Secret Weapon"**
- Key Question: Why does decision velocity matter more than decision quality?
- Core Insight: High-performing cross-border organizations optimize for speed, not perfection. The cost of a "good enough" decision made fast beats a "perfect" decision made late.
- Data: Top-quartile organizations make Type 2 decisions 3.2x faster than bottom-quartile.
- One Thing: Classify your next 5 decisions as Type 1 (irreversible) or Type 2 (reversible). Apply different governance to each.

**A-03: "Who Actually Decides?"**
- Key Question: Do you know who has decision authority in your cross-border organization?
- Core Insight: Decision-rights ambiguity is the #1 source of cross-border friction. Most organizations have RACI matrices that don't match reality.
- Data: 62% of cross-border leaders report "unclear who decides" as a weekly frustration.
- One Thing: Pick your top 3 cross-border decisions. Ask 3 people involved: "Who decides?" If you get 3 different answers, you've found your bottleneck.

**A-04: "The Localization Trap"**
- Key Question: How much should you localize — and when does localization go too far?
- Core Insight: Localization isn't binary. High-performers use "calibrated localization" — different rules for different decision domains. The mistake is applying the same rule to everything.
- Data: Organizations with calibrated localization score 28% higher on governance health than those with blanket policies.
- One Thing: For each decision domain (strategy, operations, talent, finance, compliance), ask: "Should this be HQ, local, or co-decided?"

**A-05: "What Your Board Doesn't Know"**
- Key Question: Is your board getting the right information about governance health?
- Core Insight: Most board packs are 80% financial data and 5% governance data. The information asymmetry between local management and the board is the #1 governance risk.
- Data: Only 15% of boards receive a governance health scorecard.
- One Thing: Add one page to your next board pack: "Governance Health Dashboard" — 5 metrics, 5 traffic lights.

### TRACK B: "Bridging the Divide" (10 episodes)

**B-01: "Your Team Doesn't Have a Culture Problem"**
- Key Question: Why do cross-border teams keep failing despite cultural training?
- Core Insight: Team performance is predicted by Operating Norms (β=0.34) and Alignment (β=0.29), not Intercultural Cohesion (β=0.18). Structure beats sentiment.
- Data: Only 22% of cross-border teams have written operating norms.
- One Thing: Draft a one-page "Team Operating Agreement" this week. Cover: meetings, decisions, communication, conflict.

**B-02: "Trust Is Architecture, Not Feeling"**
- Key Question: Can you design trust into a cross-border relationship?
- Core Insight: Trust has 5 dimensions (Information, Decision, Competence, Intent, Cultural). Each can be measured, diagnosed, and repaired. It's architecture, not chemistry.
- Data: Low-trust cross-border teams lose 20%+ of productivity.
- One Thing: Rate your most important cross-border relationship on the 5 trust dimensions. Find the weakest link.

- One Thing: Look at your successor. Ask: "Is this person ready for the job as it will be in 3 years — or as it is today?"
